What I Do

I’m currently a Full Stack Web Programmer at Green Mountain Software in Burlington, Vermont. I love writing great code. I love taming technologies and explaining their usefulness. Most importantly, I love to help people.

Developing Software

The past two years at a software development shop has given me great experience with a wide variety of technologies. For recent projects, I’ve written:

a React.js frontend,
Spring Boot restful APIs,
a Ruby on Rails mobile gaming backend, and
data pipelines powered by Apache Spark and MapReduce clusters.

Web apps have a lot of layers and I’ve worked on all of them. From an IT perspective, the AWS cloud is my specialty.

Maintenance and Optimization

Along with writing software, I manage customer projects; each with their own architecture. Debugging hardware, software, and the systems they’re comprised of is part of my daily routine. A more fitting job description would be full-stack(s) web developer; they’re like snowflakes. One stack in particular has been incredibly gratifying:

We were hired to build a data visualization dashboard for a large power transformer company, Dynapower. The users needed graphs without any loss of fidelity, but their raw data was too large for a browser to handle. What followed was a refactor of the data collection stack. It now consists of distributed streaming data ingestion, Apache Spark processing using point reduction algorithms, and a NoSQL database. The graphs look identical to raw data but they load in a fraction of a second; I learned a ton.

Support and Communication

Unlike other developers, I am not head down writing code day in and day out. I spend a lot of time communicating with our customers. I enjoy this part of my job very much; talking with people is a perfect complement to the actual work I do for them. Our customers have a diverse range of backgrounds (researchers, startups and small business owners) and knowing how to explain and educate to varying levels of technical know-how is an incredibly refreshing exercise.

To me, empathy has a place in software development.

I find that communicating with customers is a puzzle, unsolvable without the ability to put myself in their shoes. How much do they want to know? Do they want to fully grasp the issue or just a quick fix? Regardless, the end goal is simple and always the same: make our customers happy.

Why Support?

At Basecamp, I would thrive in a Support Programmer position. Saying “That’s not my job,” is not interesting to me - my job is to make myself invaluable. If it means learning new things, great! If it means doing things that aren’t my strong suit, I’d invite the opportunity to improve. If it involves deep mastery of a single subject or focus, then consider me excited to rise the ranks. My goal with everyone is to exceed expectations and create joy.

Having a passion for compassion, leaving people happy and going above and beyond is... me.

My experience debugging, supporting, building systems and communicating with customers primes me well for the position - and with a little help, grow into something great.

Why Basecamp?

It's simple: the passion. It seems to spill from every blog post and product and employee that comes from Basecamp. The constant questioning "What is best for our customers and employees?" is something I am committed to, and want to be a part of. Basecamp strives to make the web a better place, to make technology easy, to actually help people. It’s a place I want to call home.
